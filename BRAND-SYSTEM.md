# TGPL Digital Brand System

## Design direction

The website uses a premium engineering identity: clean editorial composition, oversized typography, precise grid systems, interactive equipment presentation and restrained motion. The visual language is inspired by industrial product websites while remaining original to TGPL.

## Approved colour palette

- **Evergreen Shadow `#043222`** — primary brand field, premium sections and key typography
- **Pantone 343 C / Deep Green `#004D3D`** — secondary surfaces, panels and gradients
- **Pantone 3405 C / Signal Green `#28B660`** — calls to action, active states and technical highlights
- **Pantone 426 C / Charcoal `#1C1C1C`** — strong text on light backgrounds
- **Pantone 427 C / Cool Grey `#D2D4D7`** — neutral panels, dividers and data areas
- **Almond Cream `#F6E9D9`** — primary light background and soft contrast

## Typography

- **Archivo** for display typography, headlines and large data
- **Manrope** for navigation, body copy, labels and forms

The hero display uses an increased line-height and separate block rows for “Powering” and “Progress” so the two words retain the moodboard scale without colliding.

## Logo system

The supplied TGPL artwork is the master identity source. The website uses:

- a horizontal lockup in navigation for legibility at web-header scale;
- the complete vertical lockup in the footer;
- the four-panel TGPL mark for favicons, application icons and equipment detailing.

The horizontal version is assembled only from the supplied artwork and does not redraw or substitute the logo typography.

## Hero experience

The homepage hero combines oversized “Powering Progress” typography with an interactive Three.js generator. The current procedural generator includes layered powder-coated panels, realistic metal and rubber materials, raised green livery, control screens, service doors, hinges, handles, louvers, cooling fan, grille, lifting beam, fuel cap and exhaust geometry.

The procedural model is an interim web asset. Approved GLB/GLTF generator, elevator, solar and MEP equipment models can be introduced later while keeping the same interaction and responsive hero framework.

## Client and partner presentation

- Selected clientele appears on an evergreen animated rail and as a structured grid on the Projects page.
- Technology partners appear on an almond-cream animated rail.
- Original logo colours are preserved inside neutral logo tiles.
- Dark or white marks receive dark-green tiles to maintain contrast.

## Interface principles

1. Use large equipment-led compositions rather than generic stock-photo cards.
2. Keep CTAs signal-green against evergreen or cream surfaces.
3. Use technical labels, fine grid lines and concise data blocks.
4. Avoid excessive rounded corners and consumer-app styling.
5. Preserve strong whitespace and high-contrast editorial hierarchy.
6. Use client and partner logos as supporting trust evidence, not as decorative clutter.
